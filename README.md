# 📘 Assistive Technology & Inclusive Education in Nigerian Secondary Schools
 
> **Academic Research | Inclusive Education | Education Policy | Nigeria**
 
---
 
## 🔍 Overview
 
This repository contains the full research project submitted in partial fulfilment of the requirements for the award of **Bachelor of Education (B.Ed.) in Guidance and Counselling** at Ekiti State University (EKSU), Ado-Ekiti, in affiliation with Emmanuel Alayande University of Education, Oyo.
 
The study investigates the **role of assistive technology (AT) in supporting the learning and participation of students with physical challenges** in selected secondary schools across Oyo State, Nigeria.
 
---
 
## 📋 Abstract
 
This study examined how assistive technology supports academic performance, classroom participation, and social inclusion for physically challenged secondary school students in Oyo State, Nigeria.
 
**Sample:** 150 respondents — 90 teachers + 60 physically challenged students (stratified random sampling)
 
**Key Findings:**
- **>70%** of respondents reported unavailability of AT across all device categories
- **62–76%** agreed that AT enhances academic performance when available
- **74–79%** affirmed AT's positive impact on classroom participation and social inclusion
- Chi-square tests found **no statistically significant** relationship between AT availability and outcomes — attributed to near-universal device absence, not AT ineffectiveness
- **Private and urban schools** had significantly better access than public and rural counterparts
**Conclusion:** AT holds recognized transformative potential, but its systemic unavailability in Nigerian schools — rooted in inadequate funding, poor infrastructure, and limited teacher training — prevents that potential from being realised.

## 🧪 Methodology
 
| Component | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Design** | Descriptive Survey |
| **Study Area** | Oyo State, Nigeria |
| **Sample Size** | 150 respondents (90 teachers, 60 students) |
| **Sampling Technique** | Stratified Random Sampling |
| **Instrument** | Structured Questionnaire (6 sections, 4-point Likert scale) |
| **Reliability** | Cronbach's Alpha = **0.86** |
| **Analysis** | Descriptive statistics + Chi-square tests (α = 0.05) |
| **Software** | SPSS |

## 📊 Key Results
 
### Hypothesis Testing Summary
 
| Hypothesis | Chi-Square (X²) | df | p-value | Decision |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| AT Availability × Academic Performance | 0.173 | 2 | 0.917 | H₀ Not Rejected |
| AT Availability × Classroom Participation | 3.014 | 2 | 0.222 | H₀ Not Rejected |
| AT Availability × Social Inclusion | 0.978 | 2 | 0.613 | H₀ Not Rejected |
| School Type × AT Availability | 4.089 | 1 | **0.043*** | **Significant** |
| School Location × AT Availability | 7.361 | 1 | **0.007*** | **Significant** |

## ⚠️ Identified Barriers
 
- 💸 **Inadequate government funding** — 70.7% reported as a major barrier
- 🎓 **Insufficient teacher training** on AT use — 72.0%
- 🔧 **Lack of technical support** for device maintenance — 71.3%
- 💡 **Unstable electricity and internet access** — 71.3%
- 🏫 **Weak management commitment** to inclusive education — 73.3%
---
 
## ✅ Recommendations
 
1. **Government Investment** — Increase budgetary allocations for AT, with dedicated funding lines and independent monitoring
2. **Teacher Training** — Mandate comprehensive AT training in pre-service and continuous professional development
3. **Public-Private Partnerships** — Formalize NGO and tech company collaboration for sustainable device provision
4. **Awareness Campaigns** — Target teachers, parents, and communities to promote rights-based inclusion
5. **Policy Reform** — Revise the National Policy on Inclusive Education to mandate minimum AT standards in all inclusive schools
6. **Counselor Empowerment** — Formally task guidance counselors with AT needs assessment and psychosocial support roles
7. **Infrastructure Development** — Ensure ramps, accessible restrooms, reliable power, and internet as prerequisites
8. **Local Innovation** — Support development of affordable, context-specific Nigerian assistive devices
