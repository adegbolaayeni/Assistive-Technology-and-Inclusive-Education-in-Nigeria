# Assistive-Technology-and-Inclusive-Education-in-Nigeria

## Overview
This project investigates the role of assistive technology (AT) in improving the learning outcomes and participation of students with physical challenges in secondary schools in Oyo State, Nigeria.

## Objective
To assess:
- Availability of assistive technologies
- Impact on academic performance
- Effect on classroom participation and social inclusion
- Barriers to effective implementation

## Methodology
- Research Design: Descriptive Survey
- Sample Size: 150 respondents (Teachers & Students)
- Sampling Method: Stratified Random Sampling
- Data Collection: Structured Questionnaire
- Analysis Tools: Descriptive Statistics, Chi-Square Test

## Key Findings
- Over 70% of schools lack assistive technology
- Strong perceived benefits:
  - Improved learning outcomes
  - Increased participation
  - Better social inclusion
- No statistical significance due to widespread unavailability
- Urban/private schools have better access than rural/public schools

## Key Insight
The problem is not whether assistive technology works — it is that it is largely unavailable in Nigerian schools.

## Challenges Identified
- Inadequate funding
- Lack of teacher training
- Poor infrastructure
- Weak policy implementation

## Recommendations
- Increase government investment in assistive technology
- Integrate AT training into teacher education
- Improve school infrastructure
- Enforce inclusive education policies

## Tools & Skills Demonstrated
- Research Design
- Survey Data Analysis
- Hypothesis Testing (Chi-Square)
- Policy Analysis
- Academic Writing
